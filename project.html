Literature Survey
in detail

Sign language recognition is the process where the gestures shown in front of any provided device are captured and then classified based on any image or video-based classification. It is a less explored area compared to other research areas. Sign language is used by deaf-mute people to communicate with other deaf-mute people. For the normal population to communicate with deaf-mute who does not know sign language, there has been much research carried out. They have mainly developed translators that identify sign language and convert it to text and convert a given text to sign language which can help both the normal population and deaf-mute population to communicate. Much such research has been carried out on American sign language. Such research has been carried out for Chinese sign language, Indian sign language, and British sign language as well. This sign language detection research has been carried out mainly based on two approaches. The first is vision based and the second is device based. Sign language detection involves several steps. Gesture detection, feature extraction, and classification are some of the steps mentioned in research papers. In the device-based approach, a system proposed in , the authors have used Microsoft Kinect sensors. This is a real-time Mexican sign language system that can translate 20 sign languages. The system that recognizes hand gestures uses an RGB-D camera to store the information of depth, color, and skeleton tracking information. The algorithm used is a dynamic time warping algorithm that is used to interpret hand gestures and for testing stages, the K-Fold Cross Validation method is used. The mean accuracy of the proposed system is 99.1%. Similarly, research proposed in uses Kinect sensors for sign language detection. According to the research, the authors have used a leap motion controller to acquire sign input. The algorithm used the LSTM model for a continuous sequence of gestures. This system can recognize 35 different sign words with an average accuracy of 72.3%.

Objective
in detail

Our main objective is to teach sign language related to animals and then to provide feedback based on the student's performance. This e-learning platform will be bridging the gap between deaf-mute children and their learning process. We will be mainly targeting primary school children with deaf-mute conditions for the research. Since the covid, there are many institutions following online education. But due to many reasons, such online systems are not available for deaf-mute schools. Therefore, this application will be fulfilling those needs. This system we build will bring hope to many parents who are struggling to teach their deaf-mute children. We aim to provide an affordable solution for deaf-mute children to learn from their homes. Since existing e-learning systems for deaf-mute children only aim to teach sign language [12], we are providing some functionalities to provide feedback on students’ learning process. This will ensure that each student properly understood the sign language. The proposed system will teach animals using some augmented reality features to simulate the real-world experience. This approach will encourage primary school students to learn new things. Since augmented reality is a trending area nowadays and kids love this kind of experience, we will provide the best solution to make this product successful.

Solution
in detail

Silent-Scholers Application can help deaf mute children to learn sign language and practise sign language. Esign can help you adjust your hand gestures and learn the correct sign gestures. With the introduction of Silent-Scholers deaf mute children can learn sign language from home.
